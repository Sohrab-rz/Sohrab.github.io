Bio

A researcher and engineer specializing in control systems, artificial intelligence, and intelligent systems, with expertise in control theory, data-driven methodologies, and dynamic systems. Currently pursuing a Master of Science in Engineering at Johns Hopkins University, I am deeply committed to addressing the challenges associated with complex systems.

Under the supervision of Prof. Enrique Mallada, my research focuses on non-parametric control (NPC) methods to develop efficient, resilient systems for real-time applications. NPC leverages expert trajectories for adaptive decision-making, prioritizing low-latency responses and computational simplicity, making it well-suited for dynamic, unpredictable environments and complex challenges.

Sohrab Rezaei is a master's student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Johns Hopkins University (JHU), specializing in Control Systems within the Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering (ECE). His academic interests focus on data-driven control systems, machine learning, and reinforcement learning. Sohrab's research explores the application of machine learning techniques in control systems and optimization algorithms for engineering applications. He has contributed to the book An Introduction to Data-Driven Control Systems and published a paper titled Comparative Analysis of Data-Driven Predictive Control Strategies. He has also contributed to the Advanced Control Systems Laboratory at JHU, led by Prof. Khaki-Sedigh. Sohrab is aiming for a Ph.D. in control systems and reinforcement learning.
