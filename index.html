Bio

I am a researcher and engineer specializing in control systems, with expertise in control theory, data-driven methodologies, and dynamic systems. I recently graduated with a Master of Science in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Johns Hopkins University. I am passionate about advancing the understanding and application of control systems, especially in complex and complex adaptive systems.

My research focused on non-parametric control (NPC) methods to develop efficient, resilient, and real-time control solutions for challenging systems. Additionally, I have worked on identifying structural properties of multivariable control systems using data, as well as control allocation strategies for over-actuated systems.

I am open to new research opportunities and practical applications in the field of control systems. I also enjoy working on developing hardware to implement control strategies effectively. Driven by strong ambition and curiosity, I am eager to learn new concepts and techniques to expand my expertise and contribute meaningfully to complex engineering challenges.
